Rules of Nine Men's Morris

Objective

  • The final goal of the game is to leave the opponent only with 2 pieces, or to block all the opponent pieces, so that they may not be moved.
  • The way of approaching to that objective is to build mills (3 pieces in an straight line), which gives the right to capture one opponent piece.

Setup

  • The board consist of a grid with 24 fields connected vertically and horizontally.
  • There are 2 players: RED and GREEN.
  • Each player has 9 pieces.
  • RED player plays first.

Gameplay

Phase I: Placing pieces

  • Phase starts with empty board.
  • Players take turns to place one by one all their pieces on empty fields of the board.
  • During Phase I there is no option to move the pieces already placed on the board.

Phase II: Moving Pieces

  • Phase starts after placing all the pieces.
  • In every turn player is moving 1 of they own pieces through one of the lines to an empty adjacent field of the board.

Phase III: Flying

  • Once a player has 3 pieces left, adjacent rule do not apply and a piece may instead be moved to any empty field on the board.

Mill

  • Mills can be built during both phases of the game.
  • Mill happens when the 3 fields in one of the lines of the board are occupied by 3 pieces of the same colour
  • The player who forms the mill must remove one of the opponentÂ´s pieces from the board.
  • Removed piece can not be used again during the game.
  • The right to perform opponent's piece removal exists every time that the mill is built.

Additional rules

  • When player's piece was moved player can not move that piece to its previous position in next move.

How to play

  • During Phase I click on empty field of the board to place new piece.
  • After forming mill click on opponent's piece placed on board to remove it.
  • During Phase II or Phase III click on your piece to select it and then click on one of the highlighted fields to move there selected piece.
